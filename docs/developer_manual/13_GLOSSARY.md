# Globe-CitySpace Engineering Handbook

# Chapter 13 — Glossary

---

## 1. Purpose

This chapter defines the terminology used throughout the Globe-CitySpace Engineering Handbook.

Its objectives are:

- standardize technical vocabulary;
- reduce ambiguity;
- simplify onboarding of new developers;
- improve communication among engineering teams;
- preserve architectural consistency.

Unless otherwise stated, the definitions presented here apply to the entire Globe-CitySpace platform.

---

## 2. Globe-CitySpace

**Globe-CitySpace** is the global exploration component of the platform.

It is responsible for:

- worldwide navigation;
- geographic location selection;
- camera control;
- scientific visualization;
- grid definition;
- area rotation;
- preparation of scientific inputs.

The Globe does not perform physical projection.

Instead, it prepares the information required by subsequent processing stages.

---

## 3. IPT-CitySpace

**IPT-CitySpace** is the engineering visualization environment responsible for displaying processed scientific information.

Its responsibilities include:

- rendering the engineering grid;
- displaying scientific scales;
- projection mapping visualization;
- interaction with the Virtual Table;
- interaction with the Physical Table.

It receives processed data from the Offline Scientific Pipeline.

---

## 4. Offline Pipeline

The **Offline Scientific Pipeline** is responsible for deterministic scientific processing.

Typical operations include:

- raster preprocessing;
- terrain extraction;
- building processing;
- grid computation;
- metadata generation;
- BMP generation;
- CSV generation.

The Offline Pipeline executes before any real-time visualization.

---

## 5. Virtual Table

The **Virtual Table** is the software representation of the physical tangible table.

It reproduces:

- actuator positions;
- cell heights;
- projection layout;
- synchronization logic;
- engineering visualization.

It allows complete validation before operating the physical hardware.

---

## 6. Physical Table

The **Physical Table** is the tangible hardware platform composed of actuated pins capable of reproducing three-dimensional surfaces.

Its primary functions include:

- physical terrain representation;
- tangible interaction;
- projection support;
- synchronized visualization;
- engineering demonstrations.

The Physical Table reproduces the processed height model generated by the Offline Pipeline.

---

## 7. Projection Mapping

**Projection Mapping** is the process of projecting digital information onto the physical surface of the tangible table.

Typical projected elements include:

- orthophotos;
- satellite imagery;
- engineering grids;
- thematic layers;
- simulation outputs;
- labels.

Projection Mapping transforms the tangible surface into an interactive visualization environment.

---

## 8. Engineering Grid

The **Engineering Grid** is a structured matrix dividing the study area into equally sized cells.

Each grid cell stores engineering information such as:

- terrain elevation;
- building elevation;
- total height;
- centroid coordinates;
- raster indices;
- actuator identifiers.

The grid is the fundamental spatial structure of Globe-CitySpace.

---

## 9. Grid Cell

A **Grid Cell** represents a single spatial sampling unit within the engineering grid.

Each cell contains:

- geographic position;
- projected coordinates;
- terrain height;
- building height;
- total elevation;
- grayscale value;
- actuator mapping.

Every actuator corresponds to one grid cell.

---

## 10. Centroid

The **Centroid** is the geometric center of a grid cell.

The centroid is used for:

- terrain sampling;
- building sampling;
- coordinate conversion;
- raster extraction;
- engineering calculations.

Using centroids ensures consistent spatial sampling across the platform.

---

## 11. Raster

A **Raster** is a regular matrix of pixels representing spatial information.

Within Globe-CitySpace, rasters are used to represent:

- Digital Terrain Models (DTM);
- Digital Surface Models (DSM);
- orthophotos;
- satellite imagery;
- grayscale height maps.

Raster datasets are processed before generating engineering products.

---

## 12. Digital Terrain Model (DTM)

A **Digital Terrain Model (DTM)** represents the elevation of the bare-earth surface.

Vegetation and buildings are removed.

DTMs are used for:

- terrain analysis;
- slope calculation;
- hydrological studies;
- engineering height computation.

The DTM provides the terrain component of the final elevation model.

---

## 13. Digital Surface Model (DSM)

A **Digital Surface Model (DSM)** represents the elevation of the Earth's surface including natural and artificial objects.

These objects include:

- buildings;
- trees;
- vegetation;
- infrastructure.

The DSM is useful when surface features must be represented in the engineering model.

---

## 14. DEM

A **Digital Elevation Model (DEM)** is a generic term describing elevation datasets.

Depending on the source, a DEM may represent:

- terrain only;
- terrain with surface objects.

Globe-CitySpace supports multiple DEM sources according to data availability.

---

## 15. Height Model

The **Height Model** is the engineering representation of elevation used by the tangible table.

For Globe-CitySpace, the default scientific model is:

```
Total Height

=

Terrain Height

+

Building Height
```

This deterministic model guarantees reproducibility across different processing stages.

---

## 16. Projection Rotation

**Projection Rotation** is the angular orientation applied to the engineering grid before generating scientific products.

The rotation angle is determined during the exploration phase and is preserved throughout the processing pipeline.

Its objectives are:

- align the study area with the engineering grid;
- simplify grid interpretation;
- maximize raster utilization;
- improve tangible table presentation.

The selected angle becomes part of the project metadata.

---

## 17. Project Rotation Angle

The **Project Rotation Angle** is the engineering parameter defining the orientation of the study area relative to geographic north.

Once selected, this value is reused by:

- the Offline Pipeline;
- projection mapping;
- IPT-CitySpace;
- the Virtual Table;
- the Physical Table.

This guarantees geometric consistency across the entire workflow.

---

## 18. Geographic North

**Geographic North** represents the direction toward the Earth's geographic North Pole.

In Globe-CitySpace, geographic north remains referenced in the project metadata even when the engineering grid is rotated.

The north arrow displayed in IPT-CitySpace reflects the stored Project Rotation Angle rather than assuming alignment with the vertical axis.

---

## 19. Scientific Metadata

**Scientific Metadata** describes the parameters used to generate engineering products.

Typical metadata includes:

- coordinate reference system (CRS);
- bounding box;
- centroid coordinates;
- grid dimensions;
- raster resolution;
- Project Rotation Angle;
- processing date;
- data sources.

Metadata ensures reproducibility of the scientific workflow.

---

## 20. Zigzag Traversal

**Zigzag Traversal** is the deterministic ordering used to map engineering grid cells to physical actuators.

Its characteristics include:

- unique actuator sequence;
- deterministic traversal;
- reproducible mapping;
- synchronization between software and hardware.

The traversal strategy is defined by the engineering contracts and remains consistent throughout the platform.

---

## 21. Engineering Contract

An **Engineering Contract** defines the rules governing communication between different software components.

Contracts specify:

- data structures;
- file formats;
- coordinate systems;
- grid dimensions;
- actuator mapping;
- metadata requirements.

Engineering contracts reduce ambiguity and improve interoperability.

---

## 22. Coordinate Reference System (CRS)

A **Coordinate Reference System (CRS)** defines how geographic positions are represented.

Typical CRS used by Globe-CitySpace include:

- EPSG:4326 (WGS84);
- projected UTM systems;
- local engineering coordinate systems.

All coordinate transformations must preserve geometric consistency.

---

## 23. Orthophoto

An **Orthophoto** is a geometrically corrected aerial or satellite image.

Unlike a standard photograph, an orthophoto has uniform scale and can be used for accurate spatial measurements.

Orthophotos are commonly projected onto the tangible table during engineering demonstrations.

---

## 24. Scientific Reproducibility

**Scientific Reproducibility** is the capability of generating identical engineering products from the same input data.

Reproducibility requires:

- deterministic algorithms;
- documented parameters;
- preserved metadata;
- controlled software versions;
- validated processing pipelines.

Scientific reproducibility is one of the fundamental design principles of Globe-CitySpace.

---

## 25. Urban Digital Twin

An **Urban Digital Twin** is a digital representation of an urban environment integrating geographic, engineering and simulation data.

Within Globe-CitySpace, the Urban Digital Twin combines:

- terrain models;
- building models;
- engineering grids;
- simulation results;
- projection mapping;
- tangible visualization.

This integration supports analysis, planning and decision-making activities.

---

## 26. Globe Navigation

**Globe Navigation** is the interactive exploration of the Earth's surface performed within Globe-CitySpace.

Typical navigation operations include:

- zoom;
- pan;
- orbit;
- camera rotation;
- location search;
- city selection.

The exploration phase precedes all scientific processing activities.

---

## 27. Area of Interest (AOI)

The **Area of Interest (AOI)** is the geographic region selected for engineering analysis.

The AOI defines:

- project extent;
- processing boundary;
- engineering grid limits;
- raster extraction area;
- metadata extent.

Every engineering project is associated with exactly one AOI.

---

## 28. Bounding Box

The **Bounding Box** is the minimum rectangle enclosing the Area of Interest.

It defines the geographic limits of the engineering project.

The Bounding Box is used for:

- raster extraction;
- coordinate transformations;
- metadata generation;
- scientific processing.

Its dimensions are recorded in the project metadata.

---

## 29. Engineering Scale

The **Engineering Scale** defines the relationship between real-world dimensions and their physical representation on the tangible table.

The selected scale influences:

- actuator spacing;
- represented area;
- projection dimensions;
- engineering interpretation.

Changing the engineering scale does not modify the underlying scientific dataset.

---

## 30. Chapter Summary

This glossary establishes the standard terminology adopted throughout the Globe-CitySpace Engineering Handbook.

The definitions presented here provide a common vocabulary for:

- software developers;
- geospatial engineers;
- researchers;
- students;
- system integrators;
- future maintainers.

Maintaining a consistent terminology improves communication, reduces ambiguity and supports the long-term evolution of the Globe-CitySpace platform.
